History

Islam began in the 7th century CE in Mecca with the prophet Muhammad. It spread rapidly across the Middle East, North Africa, and beyond, emphasizing monotheism and the message of the Quran.

Branches

Islam's two main branches are:

  • Sunni: The largest branch, focusing on the teachings of the Prophet and his companions.
  • Shia: Emphasizes the leadership of Muhammad’s descendants, especially Ali.

Practices

Core practices include the Five Pillars: Faith, Prayer, Almsgiving, Fasting, and Pilgrimage (Hajj).

Festivals

  • Ramadan: Month-long fast commemorating the Quran’s revelation.
  • Eid al-Fitr: Celebrates the end of Ramadan.
  • Eid al-Adha: Honors Abraham’s willingness to sacrifice his son.

Significant Figures

  • Prophet Muhammad: Central prophet in Islam, regarded as the final prophet.
  • Abu Bakr: First Caliph and close companion of Muhammad.
