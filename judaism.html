History

Judaism, one of the oldest monotheistic religions, traces back over 3,500 years to the Middle East. The central texts, including the Torah, recount the story of the Jewish people, guided by key figures like Abraham and Moses.

Branches

  • Orthodox Judaism: Adheres strictly to traditional practices and interpretations of the Torah.
  • Conservative Judaism: Maintains traditional values while allowing for some modern adaptations.
  • Reform Judaism: Emphasizes ethical teachings and adapts religious practices to fit modern lifestyles.

Practices

Jewish practices include prayer, observance of Shabbat, dietary laws (Kashrut), and life-cycle events like Bar/Bat Mitzvahs.

Festivals

  • Passover: Commemorates the Exodus from Egypt.
  • Hanukkah: Celebrates the miracle of the oil in the Second Temple.

Significant Figures

  • Abraham: Considered the founding patriarch of the Jewish faith.
  • Moses: Prophet and leader who received the Ten Commandments.
