History

Jainism is an ancient Indian religion founded by spiritual leaders known as Tirthankaras, with Mahavira as the last and most revered Tirthankara.

Branches

  • Digambara: Emphasizes strict asceticism, with monks who renounce all material possessions.
  • Svetambara: Monks wear white robes and follow a slightly less ascetic path.

Practices

Jains practice Ahimsa (non-violence), meditation, and a strict vegetarian diet to avoid harm to all living beings.

Festivals

  • Paryushana: A time for fasting, forgiveness, and introspection.
  • Mahavir Jayanti: Celebrates the birth of Lord Mahavira.

Significant Figures

  • Mahavira: The last Tirthankara, who reformed and promoted Jain teachings.
  • Parshvanatha: The 23rd Tirthankara, known for advocating non-violence.
