History

Hinduism, one of the oldest religions, traces back over 4,000 years in the Indian subcontinent. Centered on a rich tradition of texts, philosophies, and deities, Hinduism's historical records are found in texts like the Vedas, Upanishads, Ramayana, and Mahabharata.

Branches

Key branches of Hinduism include:

  • Vaishnavism: Worships Vishnu and his incarnations like Krishna and Rama.
  • Shaivism: Focuses on worship of Shiva.
  • Shaktism: Devotion to the goddess or Shakti.

Practices

Rituals include daily puja (prayer), meditation, yoga, and festivals honoring deities.

Festivals

  • Diwali: Festival of Lights, celebrating victory over darkness.
  • Holi: Festival of Colors, celebrating spring and unity.

Significant Figures

  • Krishna: An avatar of Vishnu, central to the Mahabharata.
  • Shiva: The destroyer god in the Hindu trinity.
