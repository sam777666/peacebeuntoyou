History

Confucianism was founded in the 5th century BCE by Confucius in China. It is a system of ethical and philosophical teachings that emphasize morality, family loyalty, and social harmony.

Branches

  • Orthodox Confucianism: Traditional teachings directly from Confucius and his followers.
  • Neo-Confucianism: A revival of Confucian thought influenced by Buddhism and Daoism.

Practices

Confucian practices include ancestor worship, adherence to rituals (li), and the cultivation of virtues such as filial piety, righteousness, and loyalty.

Festivals

  • Confucius’ Birthday: Celebrated on the 28th day of the 8th lunar month.
  • Qingming Festival: A day for honoring ancestors through visits to graves.

Significant Figures

  • Confucius: The founder and central figure of Confucianism.
  • Mencius: A philosopher who expanded upon Confucian ideas.
