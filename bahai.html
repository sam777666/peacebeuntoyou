History

The Bahá'í Faith, founded in the 19th century by Bahá'u'lláh in Persia, emphasizes unity, equality, and world peace. Bahá'u'lláh's teachings are documented in key texts like the Kitáb-i-Aqdas.

Branches

Bahá'í does not have branches or denominations, focusing instead on unity among all followers worldwide.

Practices

Bahá'ís practice daily prayer, study of the Bahá'í writings, fasting, and observance of holy days, with an emphasis on service to humanity.

Festivals

  • Ridván: Celebrates Bahá'u'lláh's declaration as the Messenger of God.
  • Birth of Bahá'u'lláh: Honors the birth of the founder of the Bahá'í Faith.

Significant Figures

  • Bahá'u'lláh: Founder of the Bahá'í Faith.
  • The Báb: The forerunner of Bahá'u'lláh.
