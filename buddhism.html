History

Buddhism began in the 5th century BCE with Siddhartha Gautama (the Buddha). His teachings, focused on overcoming suffering, spread across Asia, forming diverse traditions and schools of thought.

Branches

Buddhism has two primary branches:

  • Theravada: Oldest form, focusing on monastic life and meditation.
  • Mahayana: Includes Zen and Pure Land, emphasizing compassion and enlightenment for all beings.

Practices

Practices include meditation, chanting, ethical living, and mindfulness.

Festivals

  • Vesak: Celebrates Buddha’s birth, enlightenment, and death.

Significant Figures

  • The Buddha: Founder and spiritual leader of Buddhism.
  • Bodhisattvas: Enlightened beings who delay Nirvana to help others.
