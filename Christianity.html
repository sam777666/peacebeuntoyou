History

Christianity originated in the 1st century CE in Judea. Founded on the life and teachings of Jesus Christ, Christianity grew from a small Jewish sect to the Roman Empire's official religion by the 4th century, eventually spreading across the world with diverse branches.

Branches of Christianity

Christianity comprises three main branches:

  • Catholicism: Led by the Pope, emphasizes tradition and the sacraments.
  • Protestantism: Focuses on individual faith and the Bible. Key groups: Lutherans, Baptists, and Methodists.
  • Orthodox Christianity: Known for rich liturgy and strong emphasis on Holy Tradition.

Practices

Christian practices include weekly worship, prayer, Bible study, and sacraments such as baptism and communion. They follow teachings like compassion, charity, and forgiveness.

Festivals

  • Christmas: Celebrates Jesus's birth on December 25.
  • Easter: Marks the resurrection of Jesus, observed in spring.
  • Lent: 40-day period of reflection leading to Easter.

Significant Figures

  • Jesus Christ: The Son of God, central figure of Christianity.
  • The Apostles: Followers who spread Jesus's teachings, including Peter and Paul.
  • Mary: Mother of Jesus, revered in Christian devotion.
